Arakta

The ‘Arakta’ variety of pomegranate is presently under commercial cultivation in various regions of Maharashtra. Pre-released in the year 1989, it has now been released as ‘Phule Arakta’ for its cultivation by the Mahatma Phule Krishi Vidyapeeth, Rahuri. The ‘Phule Arakta’ variety of pomegranate is a heavy yielder and possesses desirable fruit characters. The fruits are bigger in size, sweet with soft seeds, and have bold red arils. It also possesses glossy, attractive, dark red skin. It is less susceptible to fruit spots and thrips. I am talking about the pomegranate, thought to have originated in Persia and the Sub-Himalayan foothills of Northern India. It is now produced at a commercial scale in the Mediterranean regions.

This fruit is unique in that instead of eating the bitter, spongy, white membrane inside the pomegranate, it is the tiny seeds – called arils – inside which are eaten. Each aril is a delicious sac of juice that surrounds an edible seed, ranging in colors from pink to dark red.

Pomegranates are not only delicious and beautiful but also nutritious. They are high in vitamin C and potassium, a good source of dietary fiber which aids in smooth digestion and bowel movements, and low in calories. One half cup of arils is about 80 calories but contains 5 grams of fiber. The fruit is also a good source of vitamin C, which helps the body develop resistance against infectious agents by boosting immunity. Researchers have found regular consumption of pomegranate to be effective against prostate cancer, diabetes, and lymphoma.

To choose ripe pomegranates, look for well-developed, firm, bright crimson red fruit that feels heavy for its size. The heavier it feels, the juicier it will be. Avoid spotted, over-mature fruits as they can be bitter and inedible. Also, avoid those with surface cracks, mold, and bruised or shriveled fruit as they are inferior in flavor. Once home, store them in a cool, dark place at room temperature for 5-8 days or more. Pomegranates generally possess a long shelf life and can also be stored in the refrigerator for a couple of weeks.

Follow the three-step method to retrieve the arils: First, cut off the “crown,” then score the outer layer of the skin into sections. Second, place the pomegranate in a large bowl of water and break apart the sections along the score lines. Roll out the arils with your fingers; the arils will sink to the bottom while the white membrane floats to the top. Third, strain out the water, and the arils are ready to eat and enjoy. You can purchase the arils without the shell, but they can be pricey.

Fresh arils placed in an airtight container will keep for a few days in the refrigerator. If you choose to freeze them, freeze in single layers on trays, then package in an airtight container for up to 6 months. Remove only the arils that you plan to use at the time.

There are many uses for pomegranate arils. They make an attractive garnish on salads and dishes, can be made into juice for drinking or later use in preparing soups, jellies, sorbets, sauces, and to flavor cakes, baked apples, and other desserts. Pomegranate concentrate is a popular ingredient used in Middle Eastern and Mediterranean recipes.
